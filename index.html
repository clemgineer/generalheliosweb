   General Helios, LLC. is a startup company based in Maryland. Our team is made up of five students at the University of Alabama. Over the past year, we have researched and met with engineers in the solar industry to build our knowledge base, and furthermore develop our own solution to optimize power production on a day-to-day basis. The research we perform as a team and the goals of our project were conceived by and for General Helios.

   Research: The primary object of our research is to compare the power output of the most common solar systems currently installed (rooftop polycrystalline panels) to our prototype system, the General Helios Monocrystalline Single Axis Sun Tracker. Through data acquisition and analysis, our goal is to develop superior algorithms for maximizing power production.

   Construction: Our action plan is to install a 1.2 kW single axis sun tracking solar array. Moreover, we plan to wire this system to offset the power bill of the homeowner. We are using the techniques learned in our research phase to implement both software and the IoT to give exceptional functionality and performance to our system.

   Our project has been funded entirely by a grant from the SolarCoin foundation.
